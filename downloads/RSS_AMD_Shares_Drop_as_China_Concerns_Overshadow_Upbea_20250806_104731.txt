Advanced Micro Devices Inc. dropped about 5.4% in premarket trading on Wednesday after the company was unable to give a clear outlook for resuming sales in China, a crucial market for the second-largest maker of artificial intelligence processors.
As part of its quarterly earnings reportBloomberg Terminal Tuesday, AMD declined to predict Chinese sales of the Instinct MI308, an AI processor that the company designed for the Asian country.
